Mental Health Risk Prediction using Explainable AI (XAI)

Problem: Built an early mental health risk detection system to classify Low / Medium / High risk from social media or journal text, addressing the core challenge of poor interpretability in black-box ML systems.
Approach: Designed a multimodal NLP + XAI pipeline using Multilingual BERT embeddings, behavioral feature fusion, and Logistic Regression. Benchmarked TF-IDF, SVM, and Random Forest baselines - deployed the solution with a Flask API, Streamlit dashboard, SQLite feedback loop, and SHAP / LIME / Counterfactual Explanations.
Result: Delivered an explainable decision-support system with real-time predictions, human-in-the-loop review, and monitoring — 94% accuracy with ~920 ms end-to-end latency, while the research benchmark reports Macro F1 0.6464 and Macro AUC-ROC 0.8726 for the best balanced model.

AI-Powered Research Paper Intelligence System

Problem: Researchers face information overload with growing scientific publications, and existing summarization tools fail to preserve document structure and maintain factual consistency in generated summaries.
Approach: Built a section-aware document intelligence pipeline with PyMuPDF-based PDF parsing, heuristic section detection, multi-provider LLM orchestration (Groq API, Ollama, local models), fact-consistency auditing, domain-adaptive prompting, and a custom ROUGE-based evaluation framework.
Result: Delivered an interactive Streamlit application for comparative literature analysis, survey synthesis, and publication-ready evaluation, improving ROUGE-2 F1 by 98%, factual consistency by 55%, and section coverage by 33% over baseline while eliminating factual contradictions in the final summaries.
